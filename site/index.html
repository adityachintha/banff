Parking and shuttle notes

Do not assume parking and park entry are the same thing. Parking, shuttles, reservations, and activities may be separate.

  • Banff town: know your parking target before entering town. Community advice often points travelers toward train-station parking, but verify current rules first.
  • Lake Louise / Moraine Lake: verify shuttle pickup point, ticket type, parking lot, check-in rules, and return window.
  • Lake Minnewanka and Banff Gondola: verify parking or transit before the day starts.
  • Kananaskis: verify whether your exact stops require a Conservation Pass.

Mix-and-match plans

  • Classic first-timer: Lake Minnewanka, Banff Gondola, Moraine Lake, Lake Louise, Peyto Lake, Columbia Icefield.
  • Calm Banff: Canmore base, Lake Minnewanka, early food breaks, gondola, fewer downtown stops.
  • Parking-safe: Shuttle days, preselected Banff parking, no last-minute downtown loops.
  • Bad-weather backup: Short viewpoints, Canmore/Banff cafes, museums, hot springs, and only the booked activities that still make sense.
  • Creator route: Lake Minnewanka, Banff Gondola, Moraine Lake/Lake Louise, Peyto Lake, Icefields Parkway driving clips.

Before food or crowded spots

Mountain towns get crowded fast, especially around lunch, dinner, cafes, and popular downtown areas. Before heading out, use NoWaitsy to check nearby places, avoid long waits, and make faster decisions.

Mistakes to avoid

  • Do not rely on cell signal on Icefields Parkway.
  • Do not assume Moraine Lake allows personal vehicle access.
  • Do not confuse shuttle pickup locations.
  • Do not overpack Icefields Parkway day.
  • Do not wait until you are hungry to search for food.
  • Do not assume Banff downtown parking will be easy.
  • Do not treat The Crossing as Jasper town.
  • Do not forget fuel before long scenic drives.
  • Do not plan flight-day mountain driving unless absolutely necessary.

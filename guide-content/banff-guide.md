## Route overview

Toronto -> Calgary -> Canmore -> Banff -> Moraine Lake / Lake Louise -> Icefields Parkway / Columbia Icefield -> Icefields/Jasper area -> Calgary -> Airport.

Best base logic:

- Calgary: arrival/departure buffer.
- Canmore: calmer base for Banff.
- Icefields/Jasper area: better for breaking up the long scenic route.

## Day-by-day itinerary

### Arrival Day

Toronto -> Calgary. Stay in Calgary and prioritize sleep.

### Calgary Day

Calgary. Hot air balloon and circus/event. Keep the day flexible for food, coffee, supplies, and rest. Book with your confirmed operators and screenshot the tickets.

### Canmore Transfer Day

Calgary -> Canmore. Kananaskis Heli Tour at 4:00 PM, around 20 min. Verify heli base address, check-in time, and Kananaskis pass needs before final use.

### Banff Activity Day

Canmore -> Banff. Lake Minnewanka Cruise, Open Top Touring, and Banff Gondola. This is the stacked Banff day, so protect check-in windows and food breaks.

### Lake Louise + Moraine Lake Day

Canmore -> Lake Louise / Moraine Lake. Moraine Lake and Lake Louise via shuttle. Verify exact shuttle pickup point, ticket type, return window, and parking rules before final use.

### Icefields Parkway Day

Canmore -> Columbia Icefield / Icefields Parkway. Peyto Lake, Columbia Icefield Adventure / Skywalk, and optional scenic stops. Fill fuel, download maps, and keep stops realistic.

### Return to Calgary Day

Icefields/Jasper area -> Calgary. Scenic stops with optional Zipline Mount Norquay only if timing works. Do not force extras if the drive runs long.

### Airport Day

Calgary -> Airport. Keep the day simple and stay close to Calgary.

## Parking and shuttle cheat sheet

Do not assume parking and park entry are the same thing. Parking, shuttles, reservations, and activities may be separate.

- Banff town parking strategy: arrive early, know your parking target before you enter town, and verify Town of Banff rules before final use.
- Lake Minnewanka parking/transit note: verify cruise check-in, parking, and transit conditions before final use.
- Banff Gondola/Sulphur Mountain parking note: verify parking and shuttle options before final use.
- Lake Louise parking/shuttle note: verify the correct lot, exact shuttle ticket type, and check-in rules before final use.
- Moraine Lake shuttle note: do not assume personal vehicle access. Verify shuttle pickup, return window, and backup plan before final use.
- Canmore parking/stay strategy: use Canmore as the calmer base and confirm hotel parking.
- Kananaskis pass note: verify whether your exact Kananaskis stops require a Conservation Pass.

## Canmore -> Jasper / Icefields Parkway stop guide

Must-stop:

- Peyto Lake: lake/viewpoint, 20-45 min. Iconic aerial-style lake view. Photo idea: compressed wide lake shot from the viewpoint.
- Bow Lake: lake/viewpoint, quick stop to 20 min. Easy roadside mountain-lake pause. Photo idea: foreground shoreline with peaks behind.
- Columbia Icefield: glacier/activity hub, 2.5+ hrs with booked experience. Photo idea: scale shots with the glacier and vehicles.
- Mistaya Canyon: canyon, 30-45 min. Good texture and movement. Photo idea: vertical canyon framing.

Great if time:

- Herbert Lake: lake, quick stop. Calm reflection if light is good.
- Waterfowl Lakes: lake/viewpoint, 20 min. Scenic pause without overcommitting.
- Saskatchewan Crossing: rest stop, fuel/food/checkpoint. Treat it as a logistics stop.
- Big Bend: viewpoint, quick stop. Road-scale shot.
- Weeping Wall: waterfall/roadside view, quick stop. Best as a drive-by or short pullout.
- Tangle Falls: waterfall, quick stop. Be careful around roadside traffic.

Only if going farther toward Jasper:

- Sunwapta Falls: waterfall, 30-45 min.
- Athabasca Falls: waterfall/canyon, 45 min.

Offline note: download the full Parkway area, because signal and services are limited.

## Mix-and-match plans

- Classic first-timer route: Lake Minnewanka, Banff Gondola, Moraine Lake, Lake Louise, Peyto Lake, Columbia Icefield.
- Calm Banff route: Canmore base, Lake Minnewanka, early food breaks, gondola, fewer downtown stops.

- Icefields Parkway scenic route: Bow Lake, Peyto Lake, Mistaya Canyon, Columbia Icefield, Big Bend, Tangle Falls if time works.
- Bad-weather backup route: protect booked activities, reduce roadside stops, use Canmore/Banff cafes and short viewpoints.
- Content creator route for reels: Lake Minnewanka for calm Banff clips, Banff Gondola for scale, Moraine Lake/Lake Louise for iconic views, Peyto Lake for aerial-style lake view, Icefields Parkway for cinematic driving clips.
